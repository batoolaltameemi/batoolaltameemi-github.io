About Batool


Artist and Art enthusiast

Batool Al Tameemi, born in 2004 and based in the UAE, is an artist whose passion encompasses a diverse range of artistic techniques. Currently pursuing a major in Art and Art History with a dedicated focus on Interactive Media at New York University, her artistic endeavors extend across canvases, murals, and various mediums, delving into the depths of human emotions and societal narratives. Batool's involvement in the UAE's art scene is multifaceted. Beyond her studies, she contributes as an art assistant at Sharjah Children's Centers, where she conducted over 30 art workshops that have reached and enriched more than 1000 young artists. Batool has garnered recognition through local art competitions, including the prestigious Sheikh Sultan Award and the Qasr al Hosn's Eyes of Youth contest. Batool's work has adorned multiple galleries and exhibitions also the Emirates Embassy in Riyadh. In addition to her studies and artistic engagements, Batool takes on the role of a Graphic Designer Intern at New York University Abu Dhabi's Department of Community Outreach and Student Affairs, further showcasing her commitment to creativity and community involvement.
